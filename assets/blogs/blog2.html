What is Social Media Marketing?


Social media marketing is the use of social media platforms to connect with your audience to build your brand, increase sales, and drive website traffic. This involves publishing great content on your social media profiles, listening to and engaging your followers, analyzing your results, and running social media advertisements.

The major social media platforms (at the moment) are Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, Pinterest, YouTube, and Snapchat.

There are also a range of social media management tools that help businesses to get the most out of the social media platforms listed above. For example, Buffer is a platform of social media management tools, which can help you achieve success with your social media marketing. Whether you want to build a brand or grow your business, we want to help you succeed.



Importance of Social Media Marketing


Social media marketing has different benefits for businesses. These include:

1. Increase in Brand Awareness

Social media marketing gives an opportunity to brands and small businesses to create their identity and engage with a large audience. This helps them generate more customer leads, increasing business revenue.

2. Cost-Effective

One of the greatest advantages of social media marketing is that it requires less investment in comparison to traditional advertisements like billboards or TV advertisements. It helps small businesses to grow without investing a lot in marketing.

3. Gain in Customer Trust

Increased brand awareness and engagement on social media help businesses gain the trust of consumers. It increases brand loyalty and customer retention rate.

How to Create a Social Media Marketing Strategy?


What is social media marketing without a strategy that is simple, effective, easy to implement, and goal-oriented? Here’s a step-by-step process to create a social media marketing strategy:

Step 1: Set Clear and Small Goals

You should set clear social media marketing goals. These could be engagement, brand awareness, or promotion of new products, among others.

Step 2: Figure Out Your Target Audience

Understanding your audience base is key to developing a successful social media strategy. Social media apps are no longer mere platforms where you post pictures and promote your services. They are a virtual community. You have to understand your audience’s preferences to prepare a useful social media strategy and this requires a certain amount of trial and error.

Step 3: Select Social Media Platforms

The next step is to do a social media audit. This refers to analyzing the engagement on your posts, understanding what type of content performs better, and which platforms are popular with your niche audience. For example, if you provide business consulting services, you should focus more on LinkedIn marketing.

Step 4: Brainstorm and Seek Inspiration

Now, you need to think of social media campaigns. You can seek inspiration from other brands. Ensure a consistent copywriting style across all your posts.

Step 5: Create a Monthly Social Media Calendar

It is important to create a social media content calendar to maintain consistency. This helps to get more engagement. You can also use social media marketing tools to schedule posts.

How to Do Social Media Marketing?


Learning social media marketing can be tricky, especially if you are a beginner. However, here are the core pillars that will help you understand what is social media marketing and how you can use it:



1.Strategy: Have an effective social media marketing strategy in place to achieve your goals.


2.Planning and publishing: Plan the timing and frequency of content you want to post on social media and publish videos or pictures.


3.Listening and engagement:Listen to your audience’s demands or needs by conducting regular surveys or asking for feedback. Offer them a safe space to talk. You should also engage with social media users or different brands by commenting and replying to comments.


4.Analytics and reporting: Use social media performance analyzing tools to get insights on how your content is performing, and whether you are able to achieve your goals.


5.Advertising: If your social media account is not getting enough engagement, you can also get sponsored ads to make your content reach a large audience.
